About Pitt

The University of Pittsburgh (Pitt), founded in 1787, is a leading public research university located in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Known for its strong academic programs and research excellence, Pitt offers a wide range of undergraduate, graduate and professional degrees, with standout programs in medicine, engineering, and public affairs. The campus is home to the iconic Cathedral of Learning, and its connection to the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center makes it a hub for healthcare innovation. With a vibrant student life and a strong community, Pitt is a key player in both education and research. Situated in the bustling Oakland neighborhood, Pitt provides students access to Pittsburghâ€™s cultural, professional, and recreational opportunities. The university also boasts over 400 student organizations, offering a rich array of extracurricular activities and engagement.

The University of Pittsburgh offers over 100 majors. The department of Computer Science is part of the School of Computing and Information. The computer science major requires a total of 54 credits distributed across computer science courses, mathematics co-requisite courses, and a capstone experience. A few of the programing classes students need to take include:
CMPINF 0401 - INTERMEDIATE PROGRAMMING*
CS 0445 - ALGORITHMS AND DATA STRUCTURES 1
CS 1501 - ALGORITHMS AND DATA STRUCTURES 2
They must also take classes on systems, discrete math, and statistics as well as general education classes. In addition, all Computer Science majors must complete a capstone experience prior to graduation.
Some of the faculty includes:

Dr. Jacob Biehl a professor who was previously a research assistant.
Dr. Childers who is the dean of the school of computing and information.

Student Life
