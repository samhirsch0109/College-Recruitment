About Pitt

The University of Pittsburgh (Pitt), founded in 1787, is a leading public research university in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Known for its strong academic and research programs, especially in medicine, engineering, and public affairs, Pitt offers a wide range of degrees. Guided by values of inclusion, collaboration, and community, the university fosters a welcoming environment. Its mission is to provide high-quality education, advance knowledge through research, collaborate with industry and government, and contribute to social and economic development locally and globally.

The University of Pittsburgh offers over 100 majors. The department of Computer Science is part of the School of Computing and Information. The computer science major requires a total of 54 credits distributed across computer science courses, mathematics co-requisite courses, and a capstone experience. They must also take classes on systems, discrete math, and statistics as well as general education classes. In addition, all Computer Science majors must complete a capstone experience prior to graduation.

Student Life

Prospective first-year students who are admitted through Admissions and Financial Aid for the fall term, pay their $300 enrollment fee by May 15, and pay their $325 housing deposit by their designated due date are guaranteed on-campus housing. At the University of Pittsburgh, students have access to state-of-the-art facilities like the William Pitt Union, which hosts student organizations and events, and the Petersen Events Center, home to athletic and recreational activities. Over 600 student organizations cover interests from academic clubs to cultural groups and community service. Social activities include Pitt Arts, which offers discounted tickets to cultural events around Pittsburgh. Housing options include traditional dorms like Nordenberg Hall and apartment-style living at Bouquet Gardens, with costs typically ranging from $7,000 to $10,000 per year depending on the residence.

The University of Pittsburgh’s tuition for in-state undergraduates is approximately $20,000 per year, while out-of-state students pay around $37,000. Financial aid options include scholarships like the Pitt Success Grant, federal and state grants, student loans, and work-study programs. The university offers payment plans that allow students to pay tuition in installments throughout the semester. Deadlines for financial aid applications typically align with the FAFSA deadline, while payment plan enrollment is usually required before the start of each term.

Alumni

The University of Pittsburgh’s notable alumni include Michael Chabon, Pulitzer Prize-winning author; Bob Casey, U.S. Senator; and William G. Kaelin Jr., Nobel Prize laureate in Physics. The university reports a strong job placement rate, with over 90% of graduates employed or in graduate programs within six months of graduation. Pitt's robust industry partnerships and internship programs include collaborations with companies like Google and IBM, providing students with valuable real-world experience and networking opportunities.
